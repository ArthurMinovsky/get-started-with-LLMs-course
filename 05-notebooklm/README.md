# NotebookLM — Getting Started

## What is NotebookLM?

**NotebookLM** is an AI-powered research and note-taking tool developed by **Google**. Originally called "Project Tailwind," NotebookLM was launched in 2023. Unlike general-purpose chatbots, NotebookLM is specifically designed to help you **understand and work with your own documents**.

The key differentiator: **NotebookLM only answers based on the sources you provide** — it won't fabricate information or draw on outside knowledge without your explicit permission. This makes it a highly reliable research assistant.

## What Makes NotebookLM Different?

| Feature | General LLMs (ChatGPT, etc.) | NotebookLM |
|---------|------------------------------|------------|
| Knowledge Source | General training data | Only your uploaded sources |
| Hallucination Risk | Higher | Much Lower (grounded in sources) |
| Citations | Rare | Always cites specific passages |
| Best For | General Q&A, writing | Research, document analysis |
| Privacy | Data may be used for training | Documents used only for your session |

## Supported Source Types

- **Google Docs** — Link directly from Google Drive
- **Google Slides** — Analyze presentation content
- **PDFs** — Upload research papers, reports, books
- **Web URLs** — Add websites and articles
- **Text/Markdown files** — Paste or upload plain text
- **YouTube URLs** — Analyze video content and transcripts
- **Audio files** — Upload audio for transcription and analysis

> Each notebook supports up to **50 sources** and processes up to **25 million words** of content.

## Getting Started

### Step 1 — Access NotebookLM
1. Go to [https://notebooklm.google.com](https://notebooklm.google.com)
2. Sign in with your Google account
3. Click **"New Notebook"**

### Step 2 — Add Sources
1. Click **"Add sources"**
2. Choose from:
   - Upload files (PDF, .txt, .md, audio)
   - Paste text or copy-paste a URL
   - Connect Google Drive (Docs, Slides)
   - Add a YouTube video URL
3. Wait for NotebookLM to process your sources (usually seconds to a minute)

### Step 3 — Start Asking Questions
Once sources are loaded, you can:
- Type questions in the chat interface
- Click on **suggested questions** that NotebookLM auto-generates
- Use the **"Notebook Guide"** for an AI-generated overview

## Key Features

### Chat with Your Documents
Ask questions about your sources and get answers **grounded in your content**:
> "What methodology did the authors use in this study?"
> "What are the main arguments in chapter 3?"
> "Compare the conclusions from source 1 and source 2."

Each answer includes **inline citations** you can click to jump to the exact passage in the source.

### Notebook Guide
NotebookLM automatically generates:
- **Table of Contents** — structured overview of your sources
- **Study Guide** — a quiz-style summary with key questions and answers
- **FAQ** — frequently asked questions about your content
- **Timeline** — chronological events extracted from your sources
- **Briefing Doc** — an executive summary of all sources combined

### Audio Overview (Podcast Feature)
One of NotebookLM's most unique features: generate a **podcast-style audio discussion** of your documents!

1. Click **"Generate"** under Audio Overview
2. Two AI voices discuss the key ideas from your sources in a conversational format
3. Great for commuting, review, or auditory learners

### Source Annotations
- Click on any citation to see the **highlighted passage** in the original source
- Verify every claim the AI makes against the original text
- Build trust in AI-generated summaries

## Use Cases

### Academic Research
- Upload multiple research papers on a topic
- Ask "What do these studies agree and disagree on?"
- Generate a literature review outline

### Study and Learning
- Upload textbooks, lecture notes, and slides
- Ask "Explain this concept in simple terms"
- Generate a study guide automatically
- Create practice quiz questions

### Business and Professional
- Upload industry reports, whitepapers, and meeting notes
- Ask "What are the key risks identified in these reports?"
- Generate executive summaries

### Legal Document Review
- Upload contracts, regulations, or policy documents
- Ask "What are the key obligations in this contract?"
- Find specific clauses quickly

### Content Creation
- Upload research sources before writing
- Ask "What evidence supports this argument?"
- Ensure your writing is grounded in source material

## Example Workflow — Research Paper Analysis

1. **Upload**: Add 5 research papers on a topic (e.g., climate change impacts on agriculture)
2. **Overview**: Click "Notebook Guide" → Read the auto-generated briefing
3. **Deep Dive**: Ask "What data sources do these studies use?"
4. **Compare**: Ask "Where do these papers disagree?"
5. **Extract**: Ask "List all statistical findings mentioned across these papers"
6. **Listen**: Generate an Audio Overview for a conversational summary
7. **Write**: Use the insights to draft your own document

## Tips and Best Practices

1. **Start with Notebook Guide** — Get an AI-generated overview before diving deep
2. **Always check citations** — Click every citation to verify the source passage
3. **Use specific questions** — "What percentage of..." is better than "Tell me about the data"
4. **Combine source types** — Mix PDFs, URLs, and YouTube videos for richer analysis
5. **Ask for comparisons** — NotebookLM excels at cross-document analysis
6. **Generate the Audio Overview** — Excellent for quick review before a meeting

## Limitations

- **Closed ecosystem**: NotebookLM only works with your uploaded sources — it won't answer general knowledge questions without sources
- **Source limit**: Up to 50 sources per notebook
- **No real-time data**: Cannot access live web content (must add URLs manually)
- **English-first**: Best performance in English, though other languages are supported
- **No image generation**: Text-based analysis only
