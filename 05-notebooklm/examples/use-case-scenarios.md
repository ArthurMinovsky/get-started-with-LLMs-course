# NotebookLM Use Case Scenarios

A collection of practical use case scenarios and example prompts for NotebookLM.
Use these as a guide when working with your own documents.

---

## Use Case 1 — Academic Research Literature Review

### Setup
- Upload 5–10 research papers on your research topic (PDF)
- Add relevant review articles

### Suggested Questions to Ask NotebookLM

```
1. What are the common themes across all the papers in this notebook?

2. What research methodologies are most commonly used in these studies?

3. Create a table comparing the sample sizes, methodologies, and key findings
   of each study.

4. Which studies agree with each other, and which have conflicting conclusions?

5. What gaps in the literature do these papers identify?

6. What are the limitations acknowledged by the authors across these studies?

7. Which paper has the strongest evidence for [specific claim]?

8. Summarize the theoretical frameworks used across these papers.

9. Create a timeline of how research on this topic has evolved based on
   the publication dates and findings.

10. What future research directions do the authors recommend?
```

### Deliverable
After exploring with these questions, ask:
```
Based on these sources, help me write an outline for a literature review section
covering: background, current state of research, debates, and gaps.
```

## Use Case 2 — Business Strategy Analysis

### Setup
- Upload annual reports, industry reports, and competitor analyses (PDF)
- Add relevant news articles (URLs)

### Suggested Questions

```
1. What are the key trends identified across all these industry reports?

2. What risks are most frequently mentioned in these documents?

3. Compare the market strategies described in these reports.

4. Extract all financial figures and present them in a structured format.

5. What opportunities are highlighted across these sources?

6. How do the analysts' outlooks compare across different reports?

7. Identify any contradictions between what these reports say.

8. What regulatory changes are mentioned as affecting the industry?
```

## Use Case 3 — Study Guide Creation

### Setup
- Upload textbook chapters, lecture slides (PDF), and lecture notes
- Add relevant YouTube video URLs (lectures, tutorials)

### Suggested Questions

```
1. Generate a comprehensive study guide for Chapter 3 covering all key concepts.

2. Create 20 practice questions based on these materials, ranging from
   easy to difficult.

3. What are the most important formulas or definitions I need to memorize?

4. Explain [difficult concept] using simple language and examples from the text.

5. What topics appear most frequently across these sources?
   These are likely to be on the exam.

6. Create a one-page summary of the most important points.

7. How does [concept from source 1] relate to [concept from source 2]?
```

### Generate Audio Overview
After uploading your study materials, click **"Generate Audio Overview"** for a
podcast-style conversation summarizing key topics — great for commuting!

## Use Case 4 — Legal Document Review

### Setup
- Upload contracts, terms of service, or regulatory documents (PDF)

### Suggested Questions

```
1. Summarize the main obligations of each party in this contract.

2. What are the termination clauses? Under what conditions can the contract
   be ended?

3. Identify all payment terms and deadlines mentioned in this document.

4. Are there any unusual or potentially problematic clauses in this contract?

5. What are the liability and indemnification terms?

6. Extract all defined terms and their definitions from this contract.

7. What happens in case of a dispute according to this agreement?

8. List all deadlines and milestone dates mentioned in this document.
```

> ⚠️ **Note**: NotebookLM is a research tool, not a replacement for qualified legal advice.
> Always consult a lawyer for important legal matters.

## Use Case 5 — Meeting Notes Analysis

### Setup
- Upload multiple meeting transcripts or notes documents (PDF or text files)

### Suggested Questions

```
1. What decisions were made across all these meetings?

2. Extract all action items from these meeting notes and list them by owner.

3. What topics came up repeatedly across multiple meetings?

4. What issues were raised but not yet resolved?

5. Create a project status summary based on what was discussed in these meetings.

6. What commitments did each team member make?
```

## Use Case 6 — Content Research for Writing

### Setup
- Upload research sources related to your writing topic
- Add relevant article URLs

### Suggested Questions

```
1. What are the strongest arguments supporting [your thesis]?

2. What counterarguments exist that I should address in my piece?

3. Find me the most compelling statistics and data points from these sources.

4. What expert quotes or statements could I cite in my article?

5. How do different sources approach this topic differently?

6. Suggest a structure for a 1,500-word article based on these sources.
```

## Tips for Better NotebookLM Results

| Tip | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| **Be specific** | "What percentage increase in sales is mentioned in the Q3 report?" is better than "What does the report say about sales?" |
| **Cross-reference** | "How does Source 1's claim about X compare to Source 3?" |
| **Ask for formats** | "Create a table...", "Make a bulleted list...", "Write an outline..." |
| **Verify citations** | Always click on citations to verify the actual source text |
| **Use Notebook Guide** | Start every new notebook by reading the auto-generated overview |
| **Generate audio** | Use the Audio Overview for a conversational summary you can listen to |
